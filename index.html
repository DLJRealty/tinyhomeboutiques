Explore The Region

Your Gateway to Colorado's Best Adventures

Woodland Park sits at 8,465 feet in the heart of the Pikes Peak region. From our doorstep, you're minutes from world-class hiking, historic towns, and Colorado's most iconic landmarks.

Pikes Peak

America's Mountain is just 20 minutes away. Drive, hike, or take the Cog Railway to the 14,115-foot summit for views across four states.

Garden of the Gods

30 minutes to one of America's most stunning free parks. Towering red rock formations, hiking trails, and incredible photo opportunities year-round.

Manitou Springs

A quirky mountain arts town 25 minutes away. Natural mineral springs, boutique shops, galleries, and the famous Manitou Incline — 2,744 steps of glory.

Mueller State Park

5,000 acres of pristine wilderness just 15 minutes away. Over 55 miles of hiking trails through meadows, aspen groves, and elk habitat. Colorado's best-kept secret.

Cripple Creek

A historic gold mining town 45 minutes away with casinos, mine tours, the Mollie Kathleen Gold Mine, and a heritage railroad. Step back into the Wild West.

Colorado Springs

Olympic City USA is just 25 minutes down Highway 24. The U.S. Olympic Training Center, Cheyenne Mountain Zoo, The Broadmoor, and world-class dining await.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about staying at our boutique tiny homes in Woodland Park, Colorado.

How much do I save by booking direct?+

When you book direct through our website, you save up to 15% compared to booking through Airbnb or VRBO. Third-party platforms charge service fees of 10-15% that go straight to the platform — not to improving your stay. Book direct at tinyhomeboutiques.com and keep that money in your pocket.

Do the tiny homes allow pets?+

Our boutique tiny homes do not allow pets. This policy helps us maintain the pristine, allergen-free environment our guests love. If you're traveling with pets, we recommend local pet boarding services in the Woodland Park area.

How close are you to Pikes Peak and Garden of the Gods?+

Our boutique tiny homes are just 20 minutes from the Pikes Peak Highway entrance and about 30 minutes from Garden of the Gods in Colorado Springs. You'll have direct views of Pikes Peak from the properties — many guests say waking up to the mountain is the highlight of their trip.

Do the tiny homes have firepits?+

Yes! Three of our four tiny homes (Unit Y, Unit X, and Unit 33) have outdoor firepits — perfect for roasting marshmallows under the stars with Pikes Peak as your backdrop. Unit W features an indoor fireplace for cozy winter evenings. All units have flat screen TVs.

What is there to do near Woodland Park?+

Woodland Park is a gateway to incredible Colorado adventures. Top attractions include Pikes Peak (America's Mountain), Garden of the Gods, Manitou Springs, the Cog Railway, Mueller State Park for hiking, Eleven Mile Canyon for fishing, Cripple Creek's historic gold mines, and world-class mountain biking. In winter, enjoy nearby skiing at Breckenridge, Monarch, and Ski Cooper.

What makes you different from other Airbnbs?+

We're Airbnb Superhosts and Guest Favorites with over 500 five-star reviews. Every property is personally curated with boutique-quality interiors and thoughtful touches you won't find in cookie-cutter rentals. We offer 24/7 guest support from real people and the best prices when you book direct — guaranteed.

How many guests can each tiny home accommodate?+

Our boutique tiny homes accommodate 2-6 guests depending on the property. They're perfect for couples on romantic getaways, small families, and friends looking for a cozy mountain escape. Each home is designed to maximize space while maintaining a luxurious, boutique feel.

Is Woodland Park a good base for visiting Colorado Springs?+

Absolutely! Woodland Park is just 25 minutes from downtown Colorado Springs via Highway 24, making it the perfect mountain base camp. You get the peace and views of the mountains with easy access to attractions like Garden of the Gods, the Olympic Training Center, Cheyenne Mountain Zoo, and the Broadmoor.
