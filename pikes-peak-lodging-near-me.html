Why Woodland Park Is the Best Base Camp

When planning a trip to Pikes Peak, most visitors default to staying in Colorado Springs. But experienced Colorado travelers know the real secret: Woodland Park is the ultimate base camp for a Pikes Peak adventure.

Sitting at 8,465 feet elevation on the west side of Pikes Peak, Woodland Park offers something Colorado Springs simply can't: direct, unobstructed views of America's Mountain from your front door. While Colorado Springs looks up at the mountain from below, Woodland Park faces it eye-to-eye.

The town is just 20 minutes from the Pikes Peak Highway entrance, 25 minutes from downtown Colorado Springs, and 30 minutes from Garden of the Gods. You get the best of both worlds: mountain tranquility with easy access to city attractions.

Woodland Park vs. Colorado Springs

  • Elevation: 8,465 ft vs. 6,035 ft — you're already in the mountains
  • Pikes Peak views: Direct, panoramic vs. looking up from a distance
  • Noise: Quiet mountain town vs. busy city
  • To Pikes Peak Highway: 20 min vs. 30 min (closer!)
  • Rates: Better value than downtown Colorado Springs hotels

Types of Lodging Near Pikes Peak

There are several lodging options near Pikes Peak, each with trade-offs:

Hotels & Motels

Colorado Springs has plenty of chain hotels, but they come with chain hotel downsides: no kitchen, no privacy, no character, and typically $150-300/night during peak season. You won't get mountain views from a Days Inn parking lot.

Traditional Vacation Rentals

Airbnb and VRBO list hundreds of properties in the Pikes Peak region. Quality varies wildly. Many charge Airbnb's 14% guest service fee on top of the nightly rate, which can add $30-50/night to your total.

Cabins & Lodges

Mountain cabins sound romantic but often mean dated interiors, questionable plumbing, and surprisingly high prices. Most start at $200/night.

Boutique Tiny Homes (Our Pick)

A newer, smarter option: boutique tiny homes combine the charm of a cabin with modern amenities, at a fraction of the cost. Our tiny homes in Woodland Park start at just $56/night with full kitchens, flat screen TVs, firepits, and — most importantly — direct Pikes Peak views.

When to Visit Pikes Peak

Summer (June-August)

Peak season. Pikes Peak Highway is fully open, hiking is at its best, and wildflowers blanket the meadows. Book early — this is the busiest time. Highs in Woodland Park reach a comfortable 75-80°F.

Fall (September-October)

Colorado's best-kept secret. Aspens turn gold, crowds thin out, and rates drop. September is often the best month to visit Pikes Peak. Our firepits are perfect for cool fall evenings.

Winter (November-March)

Pikes Peak Highway closes above Glen Cove (mile 13) but the lower sections offer stunning winter views. Nearby skiing at Breckenridge (2 hrs), Monarch (1.5 hrs), and Ski Cooper. Cozy up by Unit W's fireplace after a day on the slopes.

Spring (April-May)

Shoulder season with great rates. Wildlife is most active. Pikes Peak Highway begins reopening in May. Garden of the Gods is gorgeous with spring light.

Insider Tips for Your Pikes Peak Trip

  1. Drive Pikes Peak early. The highway opens at 7:30 AM. Go early to avoid afternoon thunderstorms and crowds.
  2. Acclimate first. Woodland Park sits at 8,465 ft. Spend a day at elevation before attempting the 14,115 ft summit to avoid altitude sickness.
  3. Bring layers. The summit is 30-40°F colder than Woodland Park, even in summer.
  4. Cook breakfast in. Our tiny homes have full kitchens — make coffee and enjoy Pikes Peak from your window instead of waiting in a breakfast line.
  5. Hit Garden of the Gods at sunrise. It opens at 5 AM in summer. The red rocks glow at sunrise with almost no one there.
