The Hidden Cost of Booking Through Airbnb

Airbnb revolutionized travel, and we are grateful for the platform that helped us build our business. But the reality is that Airbnb's fee structure has grown significantly over the years, and guests are now paying more than necessary for the same accommodation.

How Airbnb Fees Work

When you book on Airbnb, three separate charges make up your total:

  1. Nightly rate — Set by the host (this is the headline number you see in search results)
  2. Cleaning fee — Set by the host to cover professional cleaning between guests
  3. Airbnb service fee — Charged by Airbnb, typically 10-14% of the subtotal. This is the hidden cost most travelers overlook until they reach the checkout page.

That service fee adds up fast. On a 3-night stay at $100/night, Airbnb's service fee alone can be $40-$55. On a week-long stay, it can easily exceed $100. That money goes straight to Airbnb's shareholders — it does not improve your check-in experience, clean the property, or maintain the firepit you will enjoy under the stars.

Why Hosts Cannot Simply Lower Airbnb Prices

You might wonder why hosts do not just lower their Airbnb prices to offset the guest fee. The answer is that Airbnb also charges hosts a fee (typically 3%), and their search algorithm penalizes listings that price significantly below market. Hosts are caught in a system that optimizes for the platform's revenue at the expense of both the host and the guest. Direct booking breaks this cycle entirely.
